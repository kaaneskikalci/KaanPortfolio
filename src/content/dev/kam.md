Five of us took on this project to graduate, and it showed us just how hard — at
times almost impossible — it is to build a game of this scale in a short time.
Because it was a group project, not everyone is always at the same level of
availability or productivity, and keeping all five of us coordinated and moving
toward the same goal turned out to be even harder than I expected. But no matter
how much we struggled, in the end we made something real.

Being the solo developer on a game with this many systems was genuinely
exhausting and time-consuming. Even so, I feel I grew enormously through the
process, and building KAM was hugely valuable for me and for my team.
